Poverty-Achievement Index

A Daily Herald and WBEZ analysis of the link between poverty and school achievement

In examining elementary and high school test results, we found a strong correlation between a school's percentage of low-income students and composite test results. These charts will be updated when 2015 results become available.

Elementary school results

The interactive graphic below demonstrates how deeply stratified Illinois schools are by income. It shows the average composite meets/exceeds score for each income range, with 0-9.9% being elementary schools with less than 10% of their student population qualifying as low-income.

Mouse over the chart to see results for each income level by year.

Note: The shaded areas in the chart indicate differences in the elementary tests. Minimum ISAT standards for elementary schools were adjusted by the state starting in 2013. In 2015, the PARCC test replaced the ISATS.

High school results

High school scores show stratification in the same way as elementary schools.

In 2015, the PARCC test replaced the PSAE. Because of a smaller sample size, we divided high schools into eight groups instead of 10, with 0-12.4% being schools with less than 12.5% of their student population qualifying as low-income. Minimum standards were not adjusted for high schools as they were with for elementary schools.
